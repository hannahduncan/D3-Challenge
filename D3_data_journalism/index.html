Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Poverty

As shown in the plot above, as the poverty rate increases, there is a corresponding incease in the percent of the population that lacks healthcare. This suggests that in the United States, the costs associated with medical visits are not affordable. Alternately, this could mean that certain U.S. populations face a so-called "healthcare desert" and lack access to medical offices, hospitals, and clinics in their region.

What do we do about it? Because many of these "healthcare deserts" are located in rural regions of the US, healthcare education systems are encouraging the movement of new doctors, nurses, and radiologists to rural locations. They do this by offering early medical school admission and a generous scholarship on the condition that participants spend at least 1 year practicing in a rural area of the US.
